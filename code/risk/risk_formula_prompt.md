I have a dataset in CSV format containing information on over 100,000 behavioral health (BH) providers. Each provider is described by over 100 columns, detailing geo-demographics, billing activity, and practice behavior. This includes data like Provider Type, State, Region, In-Network Status, and various billing details (e.g., payments for diagnostics, treatment duration, concurrent treatment, crisis add-ons).

A significant portion of these providers (approximately 2500 out of 111,000) are considered 'labeled,' meaning they have additional 'action' data based on manual risk assessments by insurance experts. This action data is present in up to six columns: Comments, Most Recent Case Status, Reasons for Pass, Most Recent Case Open Date, Most Recent Case Close Date, and Most Recent Data Mining Activity Update Date. Each of these fields has been assigned a score using an LLM to gauge their importance in risk assessment.

The challenge is to develop a formula to assign a risk score to each provider. The formula should prioritize the Most Recent Case Status, Reason for Pass Score, Comments Score, and the Most Recent Closed Status Score. It should also accommodate cases where one or more values are missing, ensuring that a risk score can still be assigned. The goal is to effectively utilize the limited labeled data to understand and quantify provider risk.
